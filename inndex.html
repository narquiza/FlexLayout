Chernobyl

Chernobyl (Ukrainian: Чорнобиль, romanized: Chornobyl) is a partially abandoned city in northern Ukraine, near the border with Belarus. It is situated in the Kyiv Oblast and lies within the Chernobyl Exclusion Zone, which was established following the catastrophic nuclear disaster in 1986.

Before the evacuation, the city had a population of around 14,000 people. While the nearby city of Pripyat was completely abandoned, Chernobyl remains partially inhabited by workers and scientists monitoring the region. Today, it serves as both a symbol of nuclear tragedy and a site of historical interest.

The name “Chernobyl” has since become globally associated with the disaster, representing one of the most serious nuclear accidents in human history.
